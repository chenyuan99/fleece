# Point & Mile Valuations

Approximate cents-per-point (cpp) estimates for major rewards currencies.
These are planning baselines, not guarantees — actual value depends on specific routes,
availability, and redemption timing. Verified against common hobbyist estimates (TPG, NerdWallet, VFTW).

---

## How to Use These Numbers

**Effective earning rate** = earn rate × cpp  
Example: Amex Gold 4X dining at 1.8 cpp MR = **7.2% effective return** on dining spend

**Break-even on annual fee:** If a card costs $500/yr in net fees (after credits), you need  
$500 / (earn rate × cpp) dollars of spend in that category to break even on the fee alone.

## Quick Comparison: Best Program Per Use Case

| Use Case | Best Program | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Business class to Japan | ANA via Virgin Atlantic or Aeroplan | Fixed award chart; no fuel surcharges |
| Business class to Tokyo (JAL) | Aeroplan or British Airways Avios | Partner coverage; distance-based for Avios |
| Delta One domestic | Virgin Atlantic or Bilt Hyatt path | Low mile prices for Delta on Virgin |
| Hyatt hotel | Chase UR or Bilt | Only programs with 1:1 Hyatt transfer |
| Marriott hotel | Avoid transferring — use cash or free night certs instead | All programs give poor value to Marriott |
| Domestic US flights | Southwest Rapid Rewards (Companion Pass) or United | Companion Pass doubles domestic value |
| Short-haul US on AA | British Airways Avios or Alaska | Distance-based pricing benefits short routes |
