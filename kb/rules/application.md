# Credit Card Application Rules

Issuer-specific rules that govern welcome bonus eligibility, approval velocity, and card limits.
These rules are enforced by issuers and largely stable year-to-year, but always verify current terms.

---

## Chase

### 5/24 Rule
Chase will not approve most of its cards if you have opened **5 or more new credit card accounts** (any issuer) in the past 24 months.

- Count: all personal credit cards from any issuer that appear on your personal credit report
- Does NOT count: most business cards (Chase Ink, Amex business, Citi business, etc.) — these typically don't appear on personal credit reports
- Does NOT count: authorized user additions on someone else's account (generally)
- Does count: store cards, secured cards, and personal cards you were approved for even if you later cancelled

Cards subject to 5/24: virtually all Chase personal cards and most Chase business cards  
Cards **not** subject to 5/24: none — all Chase cards check 5/24 as of current enforcement

**Implication for strategy:** Chase cards should generally be prioritized early in a multi-card strategy before hitting 5/24. Business cards from other issuers (Amex, Citi, Capital One) are often applied for to preserve 5/24 headroom.

### Welcome Bonus Cooldown
- Same card: must wait **48 months** from when you last received a welcome bonus on that exact card before being eligible again
- Sapphire family rule: You cannot hold both Sapphire Preferred and Sapphire Reserve simultaneously. You also cannot receive a Sapphire welcome bonus if you received one on any Sapphire card in the past 48 months.
- Ink family: Each Ink product (Preferred, Cash, Unlimited, Premier) has its own 24-month cooldown clock

### Velocity / Approval Limits
- Chase may limit approvals to **2 personal cards per 30 days** and **1 business card per 30 days** (not published, observed)
- Applying for multiple Chase cards on the same day ("double dip") is possible but approval for the second is less reliable

## American Express

### Once-Per-Lifetime Bonus Rule
Each person can receive the welcome bonus for a given Amex card **only once**, even if the card is cancelled and reapplied.

- Applies to: the specific card product (e.g., Amex Gold is distinct from Amex Business Gold)
- Pop-up warning: Before submitting an application, Amex may show a "not eligible for welcome offer" pop-up. Proceeding will result in approval but no bonus. The pop-up is not guaranteed to appear.
- Workaround: The pop-up can sometimes be avoided by applying via Amex-targeted offers (email, pre-approval) or via referral links — but eligibility for the bonus depends on your history, not the link.

### Card Limits
- **Credit cards:** Maximum of 5 Amex credit cards at once (charge cards like the Green, Gold, Platinum are technically charge cards and have a separate limit)
- **Charge cards:** No published hard limit, but Amex may restrict approvals beyond 4–5 charge cards
- **Business cards:** Separate limit from personal; typically up to 5 business credit cards

### Velocity
- Amex may decline if you've applied for **4 or more Amex cards in a 90-day window** (not published, observed)
- Amex typically pulls only one credit bureau per application and does not hard-pull if you're already a cardholder applying for a new card (sometimes soft pull only — not guaranteed)

### Financial Review
Amex may conduct a Financial Review (FR) — a detailed audit of income and spending — if unusual activity is detected. Can result in card cancellations if information doesn't align.

## Citi

### 24-Month / 48-Month Bonus Cooldown
Citi has two tiers of bonus cooldowns:

**24-month rule (most cards):** You cannot receive a welcome bonus on a card if you:
- Currently hold that card, OR
- Have closed that card within the past 24 months, OR
- Have received a welcome bonus on that card within the past 24 months

**48-month rule (Strata Premier, Prestige):** The cooldown for these cards is **48 months** from when you last received the bonus (not from when you opened or closed the card).

**Same card family:** The 24/48-month clock applies within the same card family. For example, Citi Strata Premier and Citi Prestige are treated as the same family for bonus eligibility.

### Application Velocity
- **1 card per 8 days**: Citi will typically deny a second application within 8 days of a previous approval
- **2 cards per 65 days**: No more than 2 Citi cards approved within any 65-day window
- **1 Citi card per 6 months** (personal): Some data points suggest Citi limits personal card approvals to one per 6 months

## Capital One

### Card Limit
Capital One limits personal cardholders to **2 personal credit cards** at one time. Business cards are separate.

### Velocity
- **1 personal card per 6 months**: Capital One typically declines applications if you've opened a Capital One personal card in the past 6 months
- Capital One pulls **all 3 credit bureaus** (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) for most applications — a notable downside if you're managing inquiries

### Bonus Cooldown
- No published same-card cooldown equivalent to Amex's lifetime rule
- Generally you must wait until the card is no longer in your portfolio to reapply and receive a new bonus, but specific cooldown windows are not published

## General Cross-Issuer Notes

### What Counts Toward 5/24
Cards that typically **do NOT** appear on personal credit reports and don't count toward Chase 5/24:
- Amex business cards (Blue Business Plus, Business Gold, Business Platinum, Ink-equivalent Amex business)
- Citi business cards
- Chase Ink business cards
- Capital One Spark business cards

Cards that **DO** count toward 5/24 (appear on personal credit report):
- All personal cards from any issuer
- Some business cards from certain issuers (Bank of America business, Wells Fargo business, Discover business) — verify before applying

### Hard Pull Bureaus by Issuer (general patterns, not guaranteed)
| Issuer | Common Bureau |
|---|---|
| Chase | Experian (varies by state) |
| American Express | Experian |
| Citi | Equifax or Experian (varies) |
| Capital One | All 3 (Equifax + Experian + TransUnion) |
| Bilt (Wells Fargo) | Experian |
